About Ramisha

When you become a cat owner, you're initially agreeing to a sort of cat contract. You agree to provide food, shelter and a clean litter box -- in return, you'll be gifted cat hair, private concerts at 3 a.m. and the privilege of being a warm lap on demand. If you're a cat person, you wouldn't have it any other way. As the relationship grows so do your attachment with your furball.I had my very first cat when I was in college and to be more delighted I didn't have one cat at that time but triplets of only 7 days old. I know how much sensitive a cat owner can be and to what extent the person will go for one's cat. So here I am with many tips and advices that would help you and your cat have the best life you both deserve

About Yasha

I think cats bring out the kind of inter species relationship that I am comfortable with..Cats don't give a damn about what I think and they have their own way of doing things. I respect that a lot. It is more representative of the community that I am in. I do not expect conformity and same-ness. But I do expect respect and working together in a balanced and fair way.I also like that cats when they bond with you its a special bond.. If they want to be your close friend it is really by their own choice and not by genetic design or etc. And that makes the human cat bond especially special.The strange behavior of cats never ceased to amaze me. If you don't mind the shedding, they make good companions.Cats, like human, are individuals. Each is unique, each has a unique personality. I have known cats I did not like at all, and can say the same about humans. However, I would trust the average cat a great deal more than I would trust the average human. Cats are real. They don’t lie, they don’t pretend to be something they are not. Cats repay respect with respect. Cats are my kind of people.
